How do the terms I'm using pertain to standards of priority and design and all that? Nomen is a well-used standard that I for my own work am associating with the development of third level bibliography, it having been given strength of utility already by fourth level for such purpose. Sanctum is a term I began to use when I was an AMORC Rosicrucian, and it has to do with the given existence of some type of facility by third level bibliography and the like for a milder second level agenda.

The three standard second level agendas are: Vaiṣṇavism (simple and oriental), legislation (medial), and education (complex and occidental). Sanctum can be like nomen if its possessor chooses to use it for such, but it means that, be it in some fashion that pertains to the scope of what Vaiṣṇavism is, be it by legislation of some sort, be it that its possessor has received an education teaching them the appropriate “how-to,” some type of mildness is protected by which you can evaluate and/or re-evaluate what you are making of your life.
